The origin story

Six months after launch, your original architect leaves. A new engineer adds a delete action to the ledger resource. The linter doesn't catch it — nobody wrote that rule. Three weeks later there's an incident.

The post-mortem says process failure.
The real cause is entropy.

This happens because

01

Constraints live in heads, not code. The reason this resource is sensitive exists in one person's memory and nowhere else.

02

Documentation drifts from reality. The diagram drawn in week one describes a system that no longer exists.

03

AI tools generate from training, not your domain. The copilot doesn't know your invariants. It knows what Ash looked like two years ago.

04

Change is invisible until production. Nobody knew that modifying this action would break three downstream compliance obligations.

Spec-first, always

What code can't say,
the spec-kit does.

The Ash DSL captures what — resources, relationships, policies. The spec-kit captures why. ADRs explain decisions. Regulation files track obligations. Runbooks document operations. The linter enforces that all three stay honest.

When the spec-kit is complete, the copilot rarely needs to ask a question. Context resolves ambiguity. A clarifying question is a signal that the spec-kit has a gap — not a normal state of affairs.

Not RAG over a stale index. Not training data from two years ago. Structured retrieval over live DSL introspection — the copilot reads what the compiler knows, at the moment it needs to know it.

The anti-entropy thesis

Every platform decays.
Foundry is the exception.

Software entropy is not a metaphor. Constraints drift from implementation. Documentation diverges from reality. Knowledge leaves with people. The longer a system runs, the harder it becomes to change safely.

Foundry encodes constraints as executable linter rules. Decisions as ADRs the linter validates. Compliance obligations as spec-kit entries with test coverage requirements. Entropy has no entry point — not even through AI generation — because the copilot operates within the same constraints.
